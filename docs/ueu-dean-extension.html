There's a case in front of you.

A student's name is somewhere in there. Maybe it's an academic dishonesty matter — a plagiarism flag on a research paper in a course you're not entirely familiar with. Maybe there's more going on. There usually is.

You open the record. The system gives you everything it has: status fields, timestamps, account references, a subject line that condenses what happened into eight words. It's a lot of information. None of it tells you how the student is doing in the course.

So you open another tab. You search Canvas. You try to find the right course section. If you find it, you navigate to the gradebook. You note down what you see somewhere — a sticky note, an email draft, a field in the case that wasn't built for this. You come back to Salesforce and try to remember where you were.

The student is waiting while you move between systems that were never designed to speak to each other.

This is the small friction that isn't small. It's five minutes every case, or ten, or fifteen. It's the distance between knowing someone needs help and being able to act.
